## Fear

Every software developer has been in a situation where they froze when presented with an opportunity.

You have practiced by building several side projects, watched the tutorials and followed the guides etc.

## The setup

One day, an opportunity to build a real project happens to land in your laps and the indecision masked as fear rears
it's ugly head.

A mistake I made when trying to build out an application for a friend was going into too much technical detail.

He already had a backend service running in NestJS and he contacted me to build for him a frontend. We had a meeting to
decide whether to use plain [React](https://react.dev/) or [Vue](https://vuejs.org/), [Next.js](https://nextjs.org/), heck, even [Remix](https://remix.run/)

```md
Side Note

Remix is a beautiful and fast tool with a wonderful developer experience. Maybe in another world, I would have used it.
```

We wanted to have a monorepo, for the backend, frontend and a mobile application in the future. This was so we could
share code, types, dependencies among the projects without over duplication and it would help us in deployments.

Any one who has been in the industry long enough will tell you that we were optimizing code, when we did not even have a
single user !!

## The fall

We settled on [Next.js](https://nextjs.org/) and I went down another rabbit hole of deciding which libraries I would use, for example, routing,
caching, state management, UI libraries among others.

Needless to say, I did not last long on that project, and finally moved on after a long period of switching tools when
one did not _**feel**_ quite right.

## Lessons

From that time on, whenever I have had the opportunity to build a project, I use the tool which I have knowledge in and
is the best possible one for the job.

Usually for a tiny project, I will use [Next.js](https://nextjs.org/), if it is a larger
project, [Laravel](https://laravel.com/) will always come in handy.

For ecommerce applications, I discovered [Medusa](https://medusajs.com/) which has been a lifesaver.

Moral of the story, just start, build, and solve the "imaginary problems" as they come. Most of the time, they never
even materialize.

Cheers 🍻
